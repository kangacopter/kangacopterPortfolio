House of Leaves Website Art Project

Done for a web art class, this project was designed to reflect the spirit of a favorite novel. I chose to make a tribute to House of Leaves, by Mark Z. Danielewski, that reflected the novel's eerie narrative. It is remniscent of point of click games. I utilized Photoshop to create animated GIFs and manipulate the blueprint image. SDCC vs. NYCC Infographic

This is a project to create an infographic that used collected data. As a geek and frequent comic convention traveller, I chose to compare and contrast two of my favorites: San Diego Comic Con (SDCC) and New York Comic Con (NYCC). I used Illustrator to create this full page PDF, informing viewers of attendance numbers, vendors and exhibitor numbers, badge prices over time, genres of "geekdom" represented. There is also an abstract map that illustrates that SDCC is closer to movie studios, while NYCC is closer to comic publishers. Click the image below to view the full PDF.

About

What in all of space is this? This is the portfolio of Kelly Russell, a jack-of-all-trades designer, programmer, and artist based near Louisville, Kentucky. I recently graduated from Northern Kentucky University in December 2017 with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Information Technology and a Bachelor of Arts in Media Informatics. My life-long, vehement fascination with The Internets, video games, art, and technology has lead me to a desire to... well, make the computer do the things.

I began my Internet journey as a bored middle schooler, very into online virtual pets, as my keychain pet could no longer hold my interest. I was a moderately succesful buisness owner... OK, no- I just ran a shop on said virtual pet site- that very much wanted to make the best looking shop out there. Enter my need for HTML knowledge, which would lead me to some very "interesting" Geocities personal websites with fabulous MS Paint graphics. Web 1.0... frames galore!

Just as the Internets has changed, so have I, and I now pursue what excited me all those years ago... which was making things happen in that web browser.
